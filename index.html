About Me

Prof. Prasad D. Khandekar, is a Professor of Electronics and Communication Engineering having 23 years of teaching experience and 5 years of industry experience. He is a well known name in engineering education for his leadership skills and for his efforts in improving industry readiness of engineering graduates. Presently, he is a Dean of Engineering and Technology, Director Admissions and member of Board of Management at MIT World Peace University, Pune. He has also worked as Director Industry Partnesrships He was a core team member in transformation of MIT institutes on Kothrud Pune campus into Dr Vishwanath Karad MIT World Peace University, Pune.
He is regularly invited for expressing his views on Indian Higher Education scenario at conferences organized by various Chambers of Commerce, World Education Summit, Print and Electronic media. He was inviited to ABET Symposium 2016 in Florida, USA to present his model of preparing industry ready engineering graduates. He has travelled to France, USA, Austria, Malaysia, Thailand, Sri Lanka, Singapore, Dubai, HongKong and Japan for academic and research partnerships. His statement “Universities have to be ready to compete with third party aggregators who will change the way Universities are functioning now” is being seriously discussed and debated in the higher education circle of India.

Academic Leader with proven track record of building teams that deliver academic excellence.

Prof. Prasad Khandekar received the B.E. degree in Industrial Electronics engineering from Pune University, India in 1992 and the M.E. in Electronics Engineering from Shivaji University, India in 2004. He received Ph.D. degree in Electronics Engineering from the Shivaji University, India in 2013. He has worked in the field of Industrial Automation and instrumentation from 1992 to 1997. He joined academics in 1997 as a lecturer in Sou. Venutai Chavan Polytechnic. He joined Vishwakarma Institute of Information Technology, Pune as an Assistant Professor in 2005 and was promoted to Associate Professor in 2010 and then to Professor in 2013. After serving as a Head of Electronics and Telecommunication for five years and as a Dean of Planning and Development for three years, he joined MIT Pune as a Vice Principal.
In 2017, he became the key member of the committee which transformed the MIT Institutions on Kothrud Pune campus into Dr. Vishwanath Karad MIT World Peace University, Pune. Prof. Prasad D Khandekar was appointed as Associate Dean for Faculty of Engineering and technology. Alongwith this he also appointed as Director Industry Partnerships in the new university system. He was consequently promoted as Dean, Faculty of Engineering and Technology in 2019. Presently, he is also heading Director Admissions and is a member of Board of Management of MIT-WPU. He has published 25+ research papers in international journals, international conferences and national conferences. He has also two patents and one copyright published to his credit. His research interests include energy recovery techniques, low power digital design, and CMOS design.
